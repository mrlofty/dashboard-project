🧠 Understanding AI Model Benchmarks

A plain-language guide to how AI models are compared, what the scores mean, and why you shouldn't trust any single number.

🎯 TL;DR

  • No single number tells you which model is "best"
  • Arena ELO (human preference) is the closest to "real world good"
  • Companies cherry-pick the benchmarks they win on
  • For us, tool-call reliability matters more than any benchmark
  • Cost per quality is the real metric — 90% as good at 5% the cost wins

📊 The Major Benchmarks

MMLU
Massive Multitask Language Understanding
Tests general knowledge across 57 subjects — history, maths, law, medicine, computer science. Like giving a model a university-level exam across every faculty.
Example: "What is the capital of Burkina Faso?" or "Explain the Krebs cycle"
Who cares: Everyone. It's the SAT score of AI models.
SWE-Bench (Verified)
Software Engineering Benchmark
Can the model actually fix real bugs from real GitHub repos? Not toy problems — actual production issues from projects like Django, Flask, and scikit-learn. The "verified" variant means humans confirmed the test cases are fair.
Example: "Here's a failing test in Django. Fix the bug that causes it."
Who cares: Anyone using AI for coding (us with Forge/Codex).
HumanEval
Code Generation from Descriptions
Given a function description and signature, can the model write correct code? 164 problems ranging from simple to tricky. Older benchmark — most top models score 90%+ now, so it's less useful for differentiating.
Example: "Write a function that returns the nth Fibonacci number"
Who cares: Coding use cases, but SWE-Bench is more meaningful now.
GPQA (Diamond)
Graduate-Level Google-Proof Q&A
Questions written by PhD students that are so hard you can't Google the answer. Tests deep scientific reasoning — physics, chemistry, biology. "Diamond" is the hardest subset.
Example: A quantum mechanics problem that would stump most physics grads
Who cares: Research/science applications. Less relevant for us.
MATH (500)
Mathematical Problem Solving
500 competition-level maths problems. Tests reasoning chains, not just pattern matching. A model needs to show its working, not just guess the answer.
Example: "Prove that for all integers n > 2, nⁿ > (n+1)!"
Who cares: Reasoning-heavy tasks, data analysis.
Arena ELO (LMSys Chatbot Arena)
Human Preference Rating
Real humans chat with two anonymous models side by side and pick which response they prefer. Over millions of votes, this produces an ELO rating (like chess). The closest benchmark to "which model do people actually like using?"
Example: You ask both models the same question and pick the better answer, not knowing which is which
Who cares: Everyone. This is the real-world test. But it skews toward conversation quality — doesn't test tool use or coding well.
MT-Bench
Multi-Turn Conversation Benchmark
Tests how well a model handles multi-turn conversations — follow-up questions, context retention, instruction following across several exchanges. Scored by a judge model (usually GPT-4).
Example: Ask a question, then ask a follow-up that requires remembering the first answer
Who cares: Chat/assistant use cases (directly relevant to us).

⚠️ Why Benchmarks Lie

  • Cherry-picking: Companies highlight benchmarks they win. You won't see Kimi advertising their MMLU score.
  • Training on the test: Some models may have seen benchmark questions during training — inflating scores.
  • Benchmark ≠ real use: A model that aces MATH might be terrible at writing a friendly email.
  • No tool-call benchmark: There's no standard benchmark for "can it use tools reliably" — which is our #1 need.
  • Vibes matter: Two models with identical scores can feel completely different to use.

✅ What Actually Matters For Us

  • Tool call reliability: Does it correctly invoke our tools without hallucinating parameters?
  • Instruction following: Does it do what we ask, or go off on tangents?
  • Arena ELO: Closest to "real humans think it's good"
  • Cost per quality: 90% of Opus quality at 5% of the cost = Forge's sweet spot
  • Context window: How much conversation history can it hold?
  • The vibe test: Run it in our setup and see if it feels right
